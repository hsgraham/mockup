A light vegetarian pizza made from fresh ingredients

Ingredients

  • Mozzarella
  • 1 28 oz can tomato sauce
  • Fresh basil
  • Yeast
  • 3 cups flour
  • 1 cup warm water

Steps

  1. Crust: Combine 1 package yeast and 1 tsp sugar in a large bowl. Add 1 cup warm water and let sit for 5 minutes.
  2. Add 3 cups flour and mix into dough.
  3. Coat the sides of a medium bowl in olive oil. Form dough into a ball, place into greased bowl, and cover. Set dough aside to rise for 45 minutes, preferably in a warm spot.
  4. Sauce: Place 1 16 oz can of whole pealed tomatoes in a saucepan and simmer over medium heat. Add garlic powder, oregano, and red pepper flakes to taste.
  5. Simmer until tomatoes become soft and much of the liquid has evaporated. Crush tomatoes with a stiff spatula to make a smoother sauce. Remove from heat and set aside.
  6. Preheat oven to 400Â°F.
  7. Cut mozzarella into thin slices and chiffonade basil.
  8. Knead dough back into a ball and divide into two equal parts. Oil a cookie sheet with olive oil.
  9. Spread and flatten 1 part of the dough onto the cookie sheet in a rough circle or rectangle.
  10. Brush the edges of the crust with olive oil and sprinkle with garlic powder and salt.
  11. Spoon half of the sauce onto the crust and spread thinly and evenly over the crust leaving about an inch at the edges.
  12. Cover evenly with cheese slices.
  13. Bake in oven for 10-12 minutes or until crust is crispy.
  14. Sprinkle cooked pizza liberally with basil. Slice and enjoy.
  15. Repeat steps 9-14 to make a second pizza.
